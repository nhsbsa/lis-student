There are 2 ways that you could get help to pay for your NHS costs

This is a new online service and we're only inviting a limited number of people to apply.

Apply for further help

You could get further help depending on your financial circumstances.

You may also get help paying for:

  • NHS prescriptions
  • NHS dental check-ups and treatment
  • Sight tests
  • New glasses or contact lenses
  • Repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • Travel for NHS treatment

You don’t have to be claiming benefits to apply. People on low incomes with jobs may also be eligible. We take your total household income into account as well as your council tax and housing costs.

Any help you get also covers your partner and dependent children under 19.

Prepay for prescriptions

Save money on NHS prescriptions by prepaying for them

You can pay a set price for prescriptions for 3 or 12 months, no matter how many you need.

A prescription costs £9.00 per item, but prepayment costs:

  • £29.10 for 3 month
  • £104 for 12 months
    (or 10 payments of £10.40)

You will only get help with:

  • NHS prescriptions
