Sprint 49, 50 & 51

Experimental

PAYE (Single & Partner Applicants)

Hypothesis A

We believe that accepting applications from people who receive 'Wages from a job' will be a useful feature for people who receive 'Wages from a job' as it will allow them to successfully apply digitally for help with health costs. This will increase the number of people who can apply digitally by XX%

What we changed:

  • Changed 'Before Your Start' section to grant access to 'PAYE' users (single & partner applicants).
  • Added in new sections to task list (About your income & About your partners income).
  • Added in 'PAYE' journey for single and partner applicants.

What we plan to test:

  • Whether applicants (single & partner) can successfully navigate through the 'PAYE' workflow.
  • Whether assessors are satisfied that the following journey gives them all of the required information for single and partner applicants.

What we found:

  • Assessors were happy with the suggested user flow for 'PAYE'
  • User Research showed initial confusion surrounding 'Fit Notes', but by reading the 'hint text' she then quickly understood what these were.
