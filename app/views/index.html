Sprint 52, 53 & 54

Experimental

Submitting Evidence of Earnings (Single & Partner Applicants)

Hypothesis A

We believe adding the ability to provide evidence by email OR post will be a useful feature for users who are required to submit earnings evidence as it will allow them a digital route of providing evidence other than post. This will increase the number of users allowed to claim digitally by XX%

What we changed:

  • If a user enters an email address, we give them the option of whether they would like to submit their evidence by email or post, otherwise we ask them to submit it by post. Available for both single and partner applicants.

What we plan to test:

  • Whether applicants (single & partner) can successfully choose how they wish to supply evidence of their income (if required).

What we found:

  • A few applicants who we researched probably could supply evidence by email if they needed to, but the majority still liked the perceived security of sending in evidence by post (even the demographics who we expected to be more 'tech-savy' e.g. Students). We plan on extending this research before commiting to email fully as a viable option.



Hypothesis B

We believe showing what specific evidence is required from applicants on the end end page will be a useful feature for all users who receive earnings as it will allow them to submit these as quickly as possible and not provide unnecessary delay to any potential treatment. This will decrease the number of times the assessors have to go back to applicants by XX%.

What we changed:

  • If an applicant needs to provide evidence, instead of waiting for an assessor to get in touch, we tell the claimant up front what information we need from them and allow themselves to send this in digitally or by post. This brings digital back on a par with the paper form.
  • We've also added in the functionality to save what evidence is required (and their instructions) as a PDF or print it instead.

What we plan to test:

  • Whether applicants realise the end page cannot be returned to and what their preferred method of saving this is.
  • Whether applicants (single & partner) can successfully supply the correct evidence of their income first time.

What we found:

  • Users found that having to make a choice of which method they wished to supply evidence, without knowing what was actually required, was very difficult.
  • The majority of users were confused by the huge volume of information on both the email & post screens. Suggestions included breaking it down in a chronological order of what to do next.
  • Users needed further clarification to include the digi-ref in the subject line of the email.
  • Users very concerned about being unable to return to the page. Because of this, users would like the additional option of having the list of evidence emailed to themselves as well.




Known Issues

  • Prototype only includes new / amended screens and is not intended to be a full representation of the entire service.
  • No error messages in the prototype.
  • Uses dummy data on final 'check your answer' screens.

Sprint 49, 50 & 51

Experimental

PAYE (Single & Partner Applicants)

Hypothesis A

We believe that accepting applications from people who receive 'Wages from a job' will be a useful feature for people who receive 'Wages from a job' as it will allow them to successfully apply digitally for help with health costs. This will increase the number of people who can apply digitally by XX%

What we changed:

  • Changed 'Before Your Start' section to grant access to 'PAYE' users (single & partner applicants).
  • Added in new sections to task list (About your income & About your partners income).
  • Added in 'PAYE' journey for single and partner applicants.

What we plan to test:

  • Whether applicants (single & partner) can successfully navigate through the 'PAYE' workflow.
  • Whether assessors are satisfied that the following journey gives them all of the required information for single and partner applicants.

What we found:

  • Assessors were happy with the suggested user flow for 'PAYE'
  • User Research showed initial confusion surrounding 'Fit Notes', but by reading the 'hint text' she then quickly understood what these were.
